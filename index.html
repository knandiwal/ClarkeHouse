401(k) Limits

For the year 2013, the maximum an employee can contribute (by law) to a 401(k) plan is $17,500. If you’re age 50 or over, you may be able to make an additional $5,500 “catch-up” contribution.

How much can I contribute to my 401(k)?

Year
Contribution
Catch-Up Contribution
2013
$17,500
$5,500
2012
$17,000
$5,500


Keep in mind, these are limits imposed by the Internal Revenue Service. Employers may impose limits of their own, such as a percentage of income. In any case, your annual contribution cannot exceed IRS limits. Furthermore, not all employers provide for catch-up contributions.
